The Iowa Football Legacy Campaign Project Overview

Head Coach Kirk Ferentz and the Iowa Football staff are hard at work every day, building the model Big Ten football program at the University of Iowa. Their efforts, added to the efforts of those who have gone before them, have firmly established the Hawkeye program as a leader in the Big Ten and one of the most consistently successful programs nationally.

As successful as the Hawkeyes have been, the vision for this program runs deeper than the boundaries of competition and the quest for championships. Hawkeye Football epitomizes not only seizing current opportunities to excel — but taking responsibility to ensure ongoing excellence.

A portion of that vision and legacy is complete: In 2002, the UI completed construction of the Ronald D. and Margaret L. Kenyon Outdoor Football Practice Facility, and in 2006, the UI completed an $89 million renovation of historic Kinnick Stadium, enhancing one of the greatest college football environments and home-field advantages in the nation.

The final piece of the university’s vision for Hawkeye Football is on the immediate horizon; the opportunity and responsibility to complete the Iowa Football Legacy Campaign is in our hands. The campaign will allow us to build a new, permanent indoor practice facility (already in progress) and a new Football Operations Center, creating a premier Hawkeye Football campus.

“It is our vision to provide Iowa coaches and student-athletes of future generations the resources and facilities that will allow them to continue the legacy of Hawkeye Football. The tradition has been built by such legends as Nile Kinnick, Forest Evashevski, Hayden Fry, and numerous others. We are obligated to build on the successful foundation they have put in place.”

-- Kirk Ferentz, head football coach
